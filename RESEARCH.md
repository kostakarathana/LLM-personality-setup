# Research Citations

Every question in Your LLM Flavor is grounded in published psychological research. Below are the theories and papers that inform each section, with the specific question IDs they cover.

---

## 1. Cognitive Style — "How You Think" (Q1–Q8)

| # | Theory / Paper | Questions | What It Measures |
|---|----------------|-----------|------------------|
| 1 | **Cognitive Reflection Test** — Frederick, S. (2005). Cognitive reflection and decision making. *Journal of Economic Perspectives*, 19(4), 25–42. | Q1, Q2 | System 1 (intuitive) vs. System 2 (analytical) thinking |
| 2 | **Need for Cognition** — Cacioppo, J. T., & Petty, R. E. (1982). The need for cognition. *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology*, 42(1), 116–131. | Q3, Q4 | Intrinsic motivation to engage in effortful thinking |
| 3 | **Tolerance for Ambiguity** — Budner, S. (1962). Intolerance of ambiguity as a personality variable. *Journal of Personality*, 30(1), 29–50. | Q5 | Comfort with uncertain, novel, or complex situations |
| 4 | **Growth Mindset** — Dweck, C. S. (2006). *Mindset: The New Psychology of Success*. Random House. | Q6 | Belief in malleability of intelligence and talent |
| 5 | **Elaboration Likelihood Model** — Petty, R. E., & Cacioppo, J. T. (1986). The elaboration likelihood model of persuasion. *Advances in Experimental Social Psychology*, 19, 123–205. | Q7 | Central (argument-quality) vs. peripheral (heuristic) processing of information |
| 6 | **Metacognition** — Flavell, J. H. (1979). Metacognition and cognitive monitoring. *American Psychologist*, 34(10), 906–911. | Q8 | Awareness and regulation of one's own thinking processes |

## 3. Learning & Information Processing — "How You Learn" (Q16–Q25)

| # | Theory / Paper | Questions | What It Measures |
|---|----------------|-----------|------------------|
| 9 | **Dual Coding Theory** — Paivio, A. (1971). *Imagery and Verbal Processes*. Holt, Rinehart & Winston. | Q16 | Preferred information modality: visual, verbal, symbolic, or kinesthetic |
| 10 | **Felder-Silverman Learning Styles** — Felder, R. M., & Silverman, L. K. (1988). Learning and teaching styles in engineering education. *Engineering Education*, 78(7), 674–681. | Q17 | Sequential (step-by-step) vs. global (big-picture-first) learning |
| 11 | **Depth of Processing** — Marton, F., & Säljö, R. (1976). On qualitative differences in learning: Outcome and process. *British Journal of Educational Psychology*, 46(1), 4–11. | Q18 | Deep processing (understanding why) vs. surface processing (knowing how) |
| 12 | **Zone of Proximal Development** — Vygotsky, L. S. (1978). *Mind in Society: The Development of Higher Psychological Processes*. Harvard University Press. | Q21 | Preferred challenge level relative to current ability |
| 13 | **Bloom's Taxonomy** — Bloom, B. S. (Ed.). (1956). *Taxonomy of Educational Objectives*. Longmans, Green. | Q25 | Ranked preference among factual, procedural, conceptual, and metacognitive knowledge |

Questions Q19 (example density), Q20 (analogy preference), Q22 (error correction), Q23 (context level), and Q24 (learning pace) are applied learning-preference items informed by instructional design literature broadly.

## 4. Communication Preferences (Q26–Q33)

Questions Q26–Q33 measure applied communication preferences (verbosity, tone, correction style, jargon tolerance, response scope, certainty, humor, format). These are practitioner-derived dimensions drawn from:

| # | Theory / Paper | Questions | What It Measures |
|---|----------------|-----------|------------------|
| 14 | **Communication Accommodation Theory** — Giles, H., & Ogay, T. (2007). Communication accommodation theory. In B. B. Whaley & W. Samter (Eds.), *Explaining Communication*. Erlbaum. | Q26–Q33 | How people adapt communicative behavior to their interlocutors |

## 5. Motivational & Value Profile — "What Drives You" (Q34–Q37)

| # | Theory / Paper | Questions | What It Measures |
|---|----------------|-----------|------------------|

## 7. Values & Frustrations — "What Matters" (Q42–Q45)

| # | Theory / Paper | Questions | What It Measures |
|---|----------------|-----------|------------------|

## 9. Short Response — "About You" (Q51–Q58)

Free-text self-report items providing contextual information (background, goals, learning triggers, ideal mentor, AI frustrations, use cases, identity context). These are not psychometric but provide essential personalization data.
